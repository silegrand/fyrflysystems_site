The Challenge

Public Sector Security Is Never Straightforward

Public sector organisations face a combination of pressures that private sector clients rarely encounter simultaneously: the need to protect staff and the public, strict compliance obligations, procurement rules that limit how contracts are awarded, and the constant scrutiny that comes with spending taxpayers' money.

Security decisions must be defensible, proportionate and cost-effective. A system that is over-specified wastes public funds. One that is under-specified leaves people and assets exposed. Getting that balance right requires a partner who understands the public sector environment, not just the technology.

Fyrfly Systems brings that understanding. We know how public sector estates work, how decisions get made, and how to deliver systems that will stand up to scrutiny from auditors, insurers and elected members alike.

Lone worker and staff safety Council offices, housing offices and NHS reception areas can expose staff to challenging or volatile situations requiring robust monitoring and lone worker protection
Controlling access to sensitive areas Public-facing buildings must balance open access for service users with restricted access to areas handling sensitive data, cash or vulnerable people
Complex, ageing estate infrastructure Many public sector buildings have legacy infrastructure, mixed-use tenancies and listed building constraints that require careful, experienced design
Procurement and compliance obligations Public Contract Regulations and internal procurement policies govern how contracts are awarded, requiring a supplier who understands and works within these rules
Surveillance Camera Commissioner compliance Public sector CCTV operators have specific obligations under the Surveillance Camera Code of Practice that go beyond those applying to private organisations

Access Control for Public Buildings
Most Requested

Public sector buildings present a unique access control challenge: they must be open and accessible to the communities they serve, while protecting staff, restricting sensitive areas and maintaining a clear audit trail of who was where and when. Our systems are designed to manage this balance, with role-based access, visitor management integration and real-time monitoring that gives facilities and security teams full situational awareness.

  • Zone-based access with role and time-of-day restrictions
  • Visitor management with pre-registration and photo ID capture
  • Full audit trail for data protection and insurance compliance
  • Integration with HR and identity management systems
  • Intercom and video door entry for public-facing reception points
