Why Monitoring Matters

An Alarm Without Monitoring Is Just a Noise in an Empty Building

Schools are unoccupied for over 70% of the year. NHS buildings handle emergencies at 3am. Council depots sit empty every weekend. In all these scenarios, a security system that simply triggers a local siren — with no one to hear it and no one to respond — provides very limited protection.

Professional 24/7 monitoring via an NSI or SSAIB-approved Alarm Receiving Centre transforms your alarm and CCTV systems from passive deterrents into active, responsive security infrastructure that detects, verifies and escalates in real time — without requiring a member of your staff to be on call.

Empty Buildings for Extended PeriodsSchools face long holiday periods, NHS sites have quiet night hours, and council buildings sit empty at weekends. Without monitoring, incidents go undetected and response is delayed — maximising damage and loss.
Insurer RequirementsMany public sector insurers require monitored alarm systems as a condition of cover, particularly for buildings above a certain value threshold. NSI or SSAIB-approved ARC monitoring satisfies this requirement.
Keyholder FatigueRelying on individual staff members to respond to every alarm activation — including false alarms at 2am — is unsustainable and unfair. An ARC filters, verifies and only escalates genuine events, protecting your keyholders as well as your building.
Lone Worker SafetyCaretakers, facilities staff, healthcare workers and other lone workers in your buildings outside normal hours need a safety net. Lone worker monitoring provides that — with automatic escalation if check-ins are missed.
Our Monitoring Services

Monitoring That Responds — Not Just Records

Every monitoring service we offer is delivered via NSI or SSAIB-approved Alarm Receiving Centres, staffed by trained operators around the clock. We connect your systems, manage the relationship, and handle the administration — you get the protection.

Intruder Alarm Monitoring
Core Service · Insurance Grade

Your intruder alarm panel is connected to our NSI-approved ARC via a secure dual-path signalling system. When an alarm signal is received, trained operators follow a defined response protocol — verifying the activation, contacting keyholders in sequence, and notifying the police under your site's Unique Reference Number (URN) where a confirmed intruder event is suspected.

  • NSI-approved ARC with 24/7/365 trained operator staffing
  • Dual-path signalling (broadband + cellular) for resilient signal delivery
  • Keyholder notification in agreed priority sequence
  • Police response notification via URN for confirmed activations
  • Full activation reports for every event
CCTV Remote Monitoring
Active Visual Verification

IP CCTV cameras connected to the ARC allow operators to visually verify alarm activations before escalating — dramatically reducing false alarm notifications to police while accelerating response to genuine events. Operators can view live camera feeds, challenge intruders via audio speakers and coordinate a response in real time.

  • Live camera access for visual verification of alarm events
  • Two-way audio challenge via site speakers
  • Reduces false police responses and protects your URN
  • Proactive monitoring during specified high-risk periods
Fire Alarm Monitoring
Life Safety Monitoring

Fire alarm panels connected to the ARC ensure that a confirmed fire activation results in an immediate call to the fire brigade — even when the building is empty and no one is present to make that call. For schools, NHS buildings and public estates, this is particularly critical outside normal hours when a fire could take hold before being discovered.

  • Immediate fire brigade notification on confirmed activation
  • Fault monitoring with facilities team notification
  • Panel test signals monitored and confirmed
  • Complements BS 5839-1 maintenance requirements
Lone Worker Monitoring
Staff Safety

Caretakers, site managers, healthcare workers and other staff working alone in your buildings need a personal safety net. Lone worker devices and apps provide timed check-ins, amber and red alert escalation, and GPS location tracking — with ARC operators ready to coordinate help if a check-in is missed or a panic button is activated.

  • Smartphone app and dedicated device options
  • Timed check-in alerts with escalation protocol
  • Panic button with GPS location to ARC operators
  • Compliant with HSE lone worker guidance
Keyholder & Response Management
Administration Service

Maintaining an accurate, up-to-date keyholder list is a monitoring obligation that many organisations struggle to keep current. We manage your keyholder records, update contact details and escalation sequences when staff change, and conduct annual reviews to ensure the ARC always has current, reachable contacts for your site.

  • Keyholder list management and annual review
  • Online portal to update contacts at any time
  • Multiple keyholders in priority sequence
  • Response time records and attendance reports

NSI-Approved ARCs

All intruder and fire alarm monitoring connections are made to ARCs holding NSI approval to BS EN 50518, the European standard for monitoring centres. NSI approval involves regular independent audit of ARC operations, staffing, resilience and response procedures.

Resilient, Redundant Operations

Our ARC partners operate with full geographic redundancy — a second, independent monitoring centre that takes over automatically if the primary site is affected by a power failure, connectivity issue or other disruption. Your monitoring never goes offline.

BS EN 50518 — Monitoring Centres Standard
All ARC partners hold NSI or SSAIB approval to BS EN 50518, the European standard for alarm system monitoring centres. This covers physical security, staffing, communication infrastructure and response procedures.
Police URN Scheme
Sites with a police Unique Reference Number (URN) can request a police response to confirmed alarm activations. Our ARC partners are fully integrated with the police URN scheme and manage all URN administration on your behalf.
Insurer Compliance
Public sector buildings with assets or data above certain value thresholds are typically required by their insurer to have NSI or SSAIB-approved monitored alarm systems. Our monitoring connections satisfy this requirement and are evidenced by ARC confirmation letters.
UK GDPR — Video Monitoring Data
CCTV monitoring involves processing personal data. We ensure that ARC access to camera feeds is documented in your CCTV policy, subject to appropriate data processing agreements and restricted to the minimum required for the monitoring purpose.
