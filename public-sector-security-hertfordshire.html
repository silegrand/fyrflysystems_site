Serving Hertfordshire

Public sector security in Hertfordshire

Hertfordshire County Council and its ten district councils manage a substantial public sector estate immediately north of London. The county's towns — Watford, St Albans, Hemel Hempstead, Stevenage — each have significant civic infrastructure including town centre management areas, civic halls and libraries that carry Martyn's Law obligations.

Hertfordshire's proximity to London gives its public sector organisations access to the capital's procurement frameworks alongside Hertfordshire County Council's own routes to market. The county has been an early mover in reviewing Martyn's Law compliance, partly driven by its experience managing large civic events in town centres and the county's significant conference and exhibition venue sector.

Hertfordshire's NHS estate — including West Hertfordshire Teaching Hospitals and East and North Hertfordshire NHS Trust — represents one of the largest healthcare building portfolios in the South East. Healthcare settings have specific security requirements around lone worker protection, patient movement management and controlled drug security that go beyond standard commercial specifications.

Martyn's Law: key facts for Hertfordshire public venues

  • Enforcement date: 1 April 2027
  • Standard Tier: 100 to 799 persons — written procedures and trained staff required
  • Enhanced Tier: 800+ persons — physical security measures and SIA documentation required
  • Outdoor events in scope: Any managed event admitting 100+ members of the public
  • Responsible Person: Must be a named senior officer, documented in writing
  • Free assessment: Our tool determines your tier and obligations in five minutes
