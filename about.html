Why We Exist

The public sector deserves better than it typically gets

Most security companies treat schools and councils as interchangeable with warehouses and retail parks. The procurement language might change, but the approach doesn't. Systems get installed to a price, not a standard. Documentation gets filed rather than used. And when something goes wrong, the finger-pointing starts.

Fyrfly was founded because that is not good enough. Schools have specific obligations under DfE guidance, KCSiE and Martyn's Law. NHS estates carry real safeguarding risk. Councils operate under public accountability in a way that private clients simply don't.

Every decision we make is filtered through a single question: would a governor, a head teacher, or a procurement officer be comfortable explaining this choice? If the answer is no, we don't do it.

Sector-only focus
We work exclusively with education and public sector clients. That focus means our design standards, documentation, and compliance knowledge are built for your environment, not adapted from somewhere else.
Documentation that holds up

How We Work

Six things that are non-negotiable on every project

01
No cold-selling to your staff
We don't turn maintenance visits into upsell opportunities. If something needs attention, we flag it clearly and in writing. If it doesn't, we say so.
02
Compliance language that means something
DfE, KCSiE, Martyn's Law, UK GDPR — we know what these require in practice, not just what they say on paper. Our designs are built to satisfy them, not just reference them.
03
Procurement transparency
Specifications, pricing, and variations are documented clearly at every stage. We will never quote one thing and install another, or allow scope creep to appear silently on an invoice.
04
Handover that actually transfers knowledge
Staff training is included as standard, not an add-on. When we leave a site, the people responsible for the building understand how to use what we've installed.
05
Long-term service relationships
We design systems we're prepared to maintain for a decade. That changes how we specify equipment, how we document our work, and how we approach every installation decision.
06
Direct access to decision-makers
Our clients deal directly with the people who hold technical and commercial accountability for their project. Not account managers. Not call centres. The people responsible.
