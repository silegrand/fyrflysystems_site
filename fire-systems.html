The Challenge

Fire Safety Is a Legal Duty — Not Just a Box to Tick

Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, the Responsible Person in every non-domestic building is legally required to carry out a fire risk assessment and implement appropriate fire detection and warning measures. For schools, colleges and public buildings, this obligation is absolute — and the consequences of getting it wrong are severe.

Generic, undersized or poorly maintained fire alarm systems put lives at risk and expose the Responsible Person to personal liability. Fyrfly Systems designs fire alarm systems that are correctly categorised, properly installed and independently certified — giving your Responsible Person documented evidence of compliance.

Complex Building LayoutsMulti-storey schools, extended NHS corridors, listed buildings and buildings with mixed uses all require careful zone and detector planning to achieve the required detection coverage without nuisance alarms.
Phased Evacuation RequirementsLarge schools, hospitals and multi-occupancy council buildings cannot evacuate all areas simultaneously. Phased and staged evacuation strategies require intelligent fire alarm panels that can manage complex sequences safely.
False Alarm ManagementNuisance alarms in schools disrupt education and erode staff confidence in the system. Catering areas, dusty sports halls and science labs all present false alarm challenges that require detector selection expertise to manage.
Responsible Person LiabilityThe Responsible Person must be able to demonstrate that a competent, third-party certified company designed, installed and maintains the fire alarm system. BAFE registration provides that demonstration.
Our Fire Alarm Services

Design, Install, Commission, Maintain — The Complete Fire Alarm Service

Every Fyrfly fire alarm project is carried out by BAFE-registered engineers following BS 5839-1, from initial risk assessment review through to annual maintenance and certification.

Addressable Fire Alarm Design & Installation
Core Service · BAFE Certificated

Addressable fire alarm systems that identify the exact detector or call point that has activated — not just a zone — enabling a faster, more precise response. We design Category L1, L2 and M systems appropriate to your building's fire risk assessment, specify the right detector types for each environment, and install to BS 5839-1 with full commissioning documentation.

  • Fully addressable panels for precise point-of-origin identification
  • Category L1 (full property protection), L2 (escape route protection) and M (manual only) systems
  • Multi-loop panels for large or complex buildings
  • Conventional zone systems for smaller, simpler buildings
  • Complete as-installed drawings and commissioning documentation
Detector Selection & False Alarm Management
Specialist Design

The right detector for each environment is critical. We specify optical detectors, heat detectors, multi-sensor detectors, beam detectors and aspirating systems (VESDA) based on the specific environment — ensuring fast, reliable fire detection without the nuisance alarms that plague poorly designed systems in catering areas, sports halls and science labs.

  • Optical smoke detectors for most living and working areas
  • Heat detectors for kitchens, catering and plant rooms
  • Multi-sensor detectors to minimise false alarms in challenging areas
  • Aspirating (VESDA) systems for server rooms and high-value areas
Phased & Staged Evacuation Systems
Complex Buildings

Large or multi-occupancy buildings — including hospitals, multi-storey schools and council offices with public-facing areas — often cannot safely evacuate all areas simultaneously. We design and program phased evacuation sequences that alert staff closest to the fire first, allowing orderly evacuation without the risks of a simultaneous whole-building alarm.

  • Phased evacuation programming for multi-storey buildings
  • Stage 1 (investigate) and Stage 2 (evacuate) sequencing
  • Voice Alarm (VA) systems for clear, staged instruction
  • Integration with lifts, doors and HVAC for full building response
Alarm Receiving Centre Integration
Monitoring Connection

Fire alarm panels can be connected to our NSI-approved Alarm Receiving Centre for automatic notification of the fire brigade when a confirmed activation occurs. This is particularly important for schools and public buildings that are empty out of hours, where an unmonitored fire could cause catastrophic damage before being discovered.

  • Digital communicator connection to ARC
  • Automatic fire brigade notification on confirmed activation
  • Fault monitoring and reporting to facilities team
  • 24/7 ARC staffing with trained response operators
Maintenance & Certification
Legal Requirement

BS 5839-1 requires fire alarm systems to be maintained at regular intervals by a competent person. Fyrfly provides six-monthly and annual maintenance visits, testing every detector, sounder, battery, panel function and communication path — and issuing the maintenance certificate your Responsible Person needs.

  • Six-monthly service visits as required by BS 5839-1
  • Full system test and maintenance report after every visit
  • Compliance certificates for your Responsible Person records
  • Priority emergency response for system faults
BS 5839-1 — Fire Detection and Alarm Systems for Buildings
The definitive British Standard for fire alarm systems. All Fyrfly fire alarm designs, installations and maintenance programmes conform to BS 5839-1, the standard referenced by the Fire Safety Order and required by insurers and local fire authorities.
BAFE SP203-1 — Third-Party Certification
BAFE registration under SP203-1 provides the Responsible Person with independent, third-party evidence that a competent company designed, installed and maintains their fire alarm system — satisfying the competency requirement of the Fire Safety Order.
Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005
The Fire Safety Order places a legal duty on the Responsible Person to implement fire detection and warning appropriate to the fire risk. We ensure the system category (L1, L2, M) matches the fire risk assessment and the Responsible Person's legal obligations.
Building Regulations Part B & Approved Document B
New builds and significant renovations must meet Approved Document B requirements for fire detection and alarm. We design to satisfy Building Regulations requirements and liaise with building control on all notifiable fire alarm works.
