The Challenge

Schools Face Unique Security Pressures

Educational settings must balance two responsibilities that can feel contradictory: maintaining an open, welcoming environment for pupils and the community, while ensuring that access is controlled and every person on site is where they should be.

Safeguarding obligations under Keeping Children Safe in Education place clear duties on schools to manage access, monitor their premises and respond to incidents. At the same time, the wrong approach to security can make a school feel institutional and unwelcoming.

Fyrfly Systems understands both sides. We design security that is proportionate, discreet and purposeful, with every decision grounded in safeguarding outcomes rather than technology for its own sake.

Controlling site access Managing who enters the building, particularly at peak drop-off and collection times, without creating bottlenecks
Out-of-hours protection Schools contain valuable IT equipment and are frequently targeted out of hours; robust intruder detection is essential
Fire safety compliance Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 obligations and the need to protect large numbers of children simultaneously
Digital infrastructure demands Security cameras, access readers and alarms all require reliable, high-performance network infrastructure to function correctly
Budget and procurement constraints Delivering robust, compliant security within school budgets and public procurement frameworks, including framework agreements

Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSiE)

The statutory safeguarding guidance for schools in England. Our access control and CCTV systems are designed to support your obligations around site access management, visitor control and incident recording.

ICO CCTV Code of Practice

All CCTV systems we install in educational settings are designed to comply with the Information Commissioner's guidance, including data retention periods, access controls, appropriate signage and usage policy documentation.

Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005

Fire systems are designed and commissioned in accordance with the RRO, BS 5839, and local authority fire and rescue service requirements, with full documentation for your fire risk assessment.

DfE Good Estate Management for Schools

Our approach aligns with DfE guidance on estate management, supporting responsible governors and business managers in meeting their duty of care for the physical safety of their school buildings.
