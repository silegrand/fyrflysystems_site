How It Works

A Straightforward Four-Step Process

From first contact to an active contract, we make the transition as easy as possible — whether you are switching providers or setting up maintenance for the first time.

1
Initial Conversation
Tell us what systems you have, who currently maintains them and what is and is not working. No obligation, no pressure.
2
Full Health Check
We carry out a thorough assessment of your existing CCTV, access control and alarm systems — checking performance, compliance and condition.
3
Honest Report
We give you a clear written report. If the system is in good shape, we tell you. If it has issues, we are specific about what they are and what they mean.
4
Contract or Plan
If the system meets our standards, we take over the contract. If it needs work, we build an affordable upgrade plan together before committing to anything.

What Our Health Check Covers

Before we take on any contract, we carry out a thorough assessment. Here is what we look at.

CCTV camera condition, alignment and image quality
Recording system storage, retention and playback
Access control reader function and credential integrity
Door lock operation — fail-safe vs fail-secure settings
Intruder alarm zones, detectors and communicator paths
Fire alarm panel, detectors and call points
Monitoring centre connection and keyholder currency
Compliance gaps — UK GDPR, KCSiE, BS 5839, BS 8243
Upgrade Planning

We Will Not Walk Away From a Degraded System

Some organisations we speak to have systems that have been neglected for years. Cameras that have failed. Access control that has not been updated since staff left years ago. Alarms that have not been serviced since the last provider walked away.

We do not use this as a reason to refuse a contract or to push an expensive full replacement. We work with you to understand what your budget allows and build a realistic, step-by-step modernisation plan.

Safety first, aesthetics second
We prioritise the elements of the system that affect safety and compliance before anything cosmetic. Life-safety systems get fixed first.
Phased investment over time
We break upgrades into phases that align with your budget cycles — academic year, financial year, or capital allocation rounds.
No pressure, no upselling
We tell you what needs doing and when. We do not manufacture urgency or recommend replacements that are not genuinely necessary.
