01 — Market Opportunity

A Structural Shift
in School Security

Three converging legislative drivers are forcing every school, college, and public sector estate in England to upgrade or replace their security infrastructure by 2027. The pipeline is large, the compliance deadlines are fixed, and specialist providers are scarce.

24,000+
State and independent schools in England requiring security review under KCSiE 2024 and Martyn's Law
April 2027
Terrorism (Protection of Premises) Act 2025 compliance deadline — Standard Tier mandatory for venues over 200 capacity
£2.4bn
Estimated UK public sector security systems market, growing at ~8% annually driven by legislative and cyber-physical convergence
Driver Implication Window
Martyn's Law (2025 Act) All venues >200 capacity must have a documented protective security plan, designated responsible person, and rehearsed lockdown procedures. Schools are in scope from April 2027. Now — April 2027
Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSiE 2024) Updated statutory guidance explicitly links safeguarding obligations to physical security technology, visitor management, and perimeter control — creating procurement justification at every governing body. Ongoing, annual cycle
DfE Condition Improvement Fund Capital grant funding available to maintained schools for site security improvements. Specialist suppliers on CCS frameworks can access this pipeline without open tender. Annual rounds
UK GDPR / Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 Tightened requirements on CCTV data governance, retention, and DPIA documentation — schools without compliant systems face ICO enforcement risk. Now
"The compliance window is not a soft suggestion. Martyn's Law creates criminal liability for responsible persons at standard-tier venues who fail to implement adequate protective measures by April 2027."

03 — What We Deliver

Integrated Security,
Not Bolted-Together Hardware.

Fyrfly designs and installs unified campus security ecosystems — AI-driven CCTV, intelligent access control, and secure wireless networks operating as a single platform. One management interface, one data governance framework, one accountable contractor. Built for schools and public sector estates where safeguarding, compliance, and operational continuity are non-negotiable.

AI-Driven CCTV
Edge-processed AI analytics — loitering detection, ANPR, thermal perimeter monitoring, crowd density, and behavioural anomaly — operating on 4K infrastructure with sub-500ms alert generation. All footage processing stays on-site: no cloud round-trip, no third-party data exposure. ONVIF-compliant. 31-day retention standard. ICO-ready.
Intelligent Access Control
Smart credentials (card, mobile, biometric), visitor management with pre-arrival screening, dynamic lockdown capability with single-button activation, and full Active Directory sync for staff onboarding and offboarding. Door-forced alerts auto-direct the nearest PTZ camera — one correlated event, not two separate alarms. OSDP v2. Martyn's Law compliant.
Secure Wireless Network Backbone
Dedicated security VLAN, logically separated from classroom and administrative traffic, with QoS enforcement ensuring CCTV and access control are never bandwidth-constrained by routine network use. 4G/5G failover keeps systems live during ISP outages. Encrypted mesh architecture. Device health monitoring with automated alerts.
Rapid-Deployment Solar CCTV Tower
A proprietary bespoke capability — solar-powered, self-contained surveillance towers for sites without mains infrastructure, construction phases, open grounds, or temporary deployments. Developed from the Sunstone Systems platform. No groundworks. No power supply contract. Operational within four hours. Unique in the education and public sector market.

05 — Proposed Engagement

A Structure Built
for Both Parties.

We propose a phased commercial partnership, beginning with a jointly-delivered pilot programme in Kent and the South East, with a clear framework for deepening the relationship as revenue and pipeline validate the model.

Phase 1 — Pilot
Joint delivery of 3–5 school or public sector security projects in Kent and the South East. Fyrfly leads design, specification, and client relationship. Kings provides installation engineering and ARC integration. Revenue split to be agreed; costs shared on a project basis.
Phase 2 — Commercial Partnership
Formalised partnership agreement covering lead generation, pipeline sharing, and managed service contract structure. Kings ARC as the monitoring backbone for Fyrfly-specified systems. Preferred subcontractor terms for Kings engineering on Fyrfly-led projects.
Phase 3 — Equity / Deeper Structure
Subject to Phase 1 and 2 performance, both parties to discuss equity participation, joint venture structure, or a structured investment by Kings into Fyrfly Systems. Founders retain majority control and IP ownership throughout.
IP Ownership
Fyrfly retains all IP. Granted patents and proprietary platform remain with Fyrfly Systems throughout any commercial partnership. Kings benefits from exclusive or preferred access to the platform in agreed geographies.
Geography
Primary focus South East England — Kent, London, Surrey, Essex, Sussex. Expansion to national coverage in Phase 2 subject to capacity and mutual agreement.
Next Step
A 60-minute strategic conversation between the Fyrfly founders and Kings leadership to assess alignment and agree whether to proceed to a formal pilot term sheet.
